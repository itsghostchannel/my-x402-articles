# Blockchain: The Defibrillator Journalism Needs

I'm not going to sugarcoat it: journalism is on life support.

It used to be the fourth estate — a vital organ of democracy that kept power in check and citizens informed. Today, that organ is failing. The promise of digital media has morphed into a dystopian reality where quality reporting is sacrificed at the altar of engagement metrics.

But, there's still time.  Within the tech powering cryptocurrencies and NFTs lies a set of properties that, when addressed with the right machinery operated under careful hands, could jolt the journalism industry back to life.


A Short History of Journalism’s Long Decline

Let’s unpack this mess. The journalism industry has had a long decline. Its current symptoms include:

 * Systemic Revenue Failure: Traditional advertising revenue streams have dried up, and subscription models alone can't replenish the resource supply needed to stabilize failing publications. Meanwhile, newsrooms are hemorrhaging talent and attempting to back-fill with AI.
 * Chronic Content Devaluation: Quality journalism takes time and effort, but is priced like a commodity available in limitless supply. [See our previous essay on defining The True Value of Journalism in the Digital Age]
 * Null Ownership Syndrome: Journalists work tirelessly to construct complete stories, only to lose control over their intellectual property across digital space. 
 * Terminal Trust Issues: Public trust in media is diminishing thanks to deep fakes and disinformation. Propaganda infests news feeds while fact-checkers become opaque “community guidelines.”


A Blockchain-Based Shock to Journalism’s System 

While the industry on the whole has had some sketchy moments,  its underlying tech shows promise — as a decentralized, transparent, and tamper-proof system to revive digital publishing. Here’s how I see it coming together:

 1. Ironclad Attribution: Stamp your authorship directly onto the blockchain, creating an unalterable record of creation. Content thieves: consider this your quarantine notice.
 2. Smarter Contracts: Licensing agreements that self-administer without middlemen. Set your terms, and let the blockchain handle the treatment protocol.
 3. Micropayment Transfers: Finally, a working pay-per-article system. Readers can support quality content through small but vital transfusions, even just a few cents at a time.
 4. Transparent Monitoring: Every view, share, and earned cent gets recorded on the blockchain. No more obscured diagnostics — you'll have a real-time record of your content’s access and use.
 5. AI Consent Systems: License your work for AI consumption and receive compensation for contributing to future content. Help AI work with you, and help AI companies not want to work against you.

The Recovery Plan: Challenges in the Blockchain Treatment

Let's maintain clinical honesty: this isn't a miracle cure. The path to a blockchain-powered journalism future faces several complications:

 * Adoption Resistance: We need critical mass for the procedure to work. Some journalists still think blockchain is a new type of medical record system.
 * Interface Syndrome: If it's not as intuitive as familiar tools, the crypto treatment won't take. We're working overtime on user-friendly delivery systems.
 * Regulatory Examinations: In the Web3 world, laws can move slower than hospital bureaucracy. We're aware that we’re navigating uncharted regulatory territory. And we’re committed to operating safely within it.
 * Environmental Health: We're conscious of energy concerns. Our solution focuses on sustainable protocols that won't harm the planet's health, and could even help it.

Ultimately, the potential benefits of trying to build this future far outweigh the costs of resigning ourselves to a world of web2 oblivion. We're envisioning at a future where journalism can recover its rightful role as a trusted, valued pillar of society.

At CTRL+X, we're committed to the protocols we’ve designed and are currently in the process of building. With the right application of blockchain technology, leadership, resource allocation, and community growth, journalism won't merely survive — it will make a full recovery. And, potentially evolve into something better than it’s ever been before.
